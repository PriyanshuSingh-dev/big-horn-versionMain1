Overview

Embark on a mesmerizing Himalayan adventure with the Valley of Flowers Trek in Uttarakhand — an ideal escape for trekkers, nature lovers, and photography buffs. Hidden in the scenic Chamoli district, this UNESCO World Heritage Site bursts into life during the monsoon season, blanketed with rare Himalayan blooms, rolling green meadows, and shimmering streams.

Now Open from 2nd June! Witness nature at its finest as you trek through a valley painted in vibrant colors, with misty waterfalls and breathtaking mountain vistas. This is your chance to experience one of the Himalayas’ most enchanting landscapes — truly a slice of heaven on Earth.

inclusion

  • Accommodation: Stay at a guesthouse in Lohajung village and in alpine tents during the trek (shared basis: 3–4 people per tent).
  • Meals:All vegetarian meals from Day 1 dinner to Day 6 breakfast as per the itinerary.
  • Trekking Gear: Provision of tents, sleeping bags, mats, gaiters, and microspikes for snow treks.
  • Safety:Equipped with basic first aid kits, oxygen cylinders, and guides trained in handling emergencies.
  • Trek Leader: Guidance from a certified trek leader (with Basic/Advanced Mountaineering Training).
  • Transport: Shared vehicle travel from Rishikesh to Rishikesh (start and end point).
  • Permits: All necessary forest permits, camping fees, and environmental charges included.
  • Support Team: Skilled local guides, cooks, and porters managing camp setup and logistics.

Exclusion

  • Taxes: 5% GST extra on the total cost.
  • Personal Purchases:Items like bottled water, extra snacks, and beverages outside the provided meals.
  • Insurance: Trekkers must arrange their own travel and medical insurance.
  • Porter Charges:Fees for personal luggage carriage by porters are not included.
  • Medical Costs: In case of emergencies requiring evacuation (like helicopter rescue), the cost must be borne by the trekker.
  • Other Expenses: Unforeseen costs due to weather delays, natural events, or activities not listed in the itinerary.

Things To Carry

  • Thermal Base Layers: Keeps body warm by retaining heat; choose wool or synthetic materials.
  • Insulated Jacket:: Down or synthetic jackets are essential to combat cold temperatures.
  • Waterproof Trekking Boots: Must be warm, waterproof, and suitable for snow.
  • Woolen Socks: Carry 3–4 pairs; Merino wool is recommended for warmth and comfort.
  • Gloves:Insulated, windproof gloves are vital for hand protection in snow.
  • Balaclava/Neck Gaiter:Shields face and neck from cold winds and keeps warmth in.
  • Rainproof Outerwear:Lightweight, waterproof jacket and pants to handle snow or rain.
  • Headlamp with Extra Batteries:Useful for low-light conditions; batteries drain faster in cold.
  • First Aid Kit: Include basics like antiseptic, painkillers, band-aids, and any personal medication.
  • Sun Protection:Sunglasses, SPF lip balm, and sunscreen are essential even in winter sun.
  • Personal Toiletries:Travel-friendly hygiene kit including towel, soap, wipes, sanitizer, etc.

Trek Highlights

  • UNESCO Listed Site: Celebrated globally for its untouched beauty and exceptional biodiversity.
  • Over 600 Floral Varieties:Discover stunning monsoon-season blooms like the rare blue poppy, Brahma Kamal, and cobra lily..
  • Himalayan Wildlife:Chance to see high-altitude species including musk deer, butterflies, various Himalayan birds, and—if lucky—even a snow leopard.
  • Spiritual Experience Visit a revered Sikh pilgrimage site located at 4,329 meters, next to a glacial lake—offering a peaceful, spiritual aspect to the journey.
  • Beginner-Friendly Well-marked, manageable trails and basic lodging make it an ideal adventure for new trekkers.

Itinerary

Day 0: Delhi to Haridwar (Overnight Journey)

  • Begin your adventure with an overnight journey from Delhi to Haridwar by train, bus, or private transport. Relax and get ready for the scenic Himalayan drive that follows.

Day 1: Haridwar to Govindghat (Drive – 10 to 11 Hours)

  • Start your day early with a beautiful drive from Haridwar to Govindghat, crossing through Devprayag, Rudraprayag, Karnaprayag, and Joshimath. Enjoy mesmerizing views of river confluences and the Himalayan foothills.
  • Overnight stay: Govindghat

Day 2: Govindghat to Ghangaria (Trek – 9 km)

  • Begin your trek alongside the gushing Pushpawati River. The route is scenic and moderately challenging, with well-marked paths and resting points.
  • Overnight stay in tents.
  • Overnight stay: Ghangaria

Day 3: Ghangaria to Valley of Flowers and Back (Trek – 8 km)

  • Explore the breathtaking Valley of Flowers, filled with a carpet of blooming Himalayan flowers, vibrant meadows, and mist-covered peaks. Return to Ghangaria by afternoon.
  • Overnight stay:Ghangaria

Day 4: Ghangaria to Hemkund Sahib and Back (Trek – 12 km)

  • Take a spiritually uplifting trek to Hemkund Sahib , the highest Gurudwara in the world, located beside a pristine glacial lake at 4,329 m. This challenging climb is both rewarding and inspiring.
  • Overnight stay: Ghangaria

Day 5: Ghangaria to Govindghat (Trek), Drive to Joshimath

  • Trek downhill to Govindghat and take a short drive to Joshimath. Unwind and explore this peaceful hill town.
  • Overnight stay: Joshimath

Day 6: Joshimath to Haridwar (Drive – 10 to 11 Hours)

  • Return to Haridwar through the same scenic mountain route. The drive is filled with incredible views, perfect for some final photos and reflection on the trek.
