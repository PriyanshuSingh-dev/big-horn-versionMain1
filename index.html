Frequently Asked
Question

What documents do I need for the trek?

Valid government-issued photo ID, medical certificate (if required), and signed indemnity form.

What fitness level is required for trekking?

Basic fitness with ability to walk 5-6 hours daily. Start cardio training 4-6 weeks before your trek and practice walking with a backpack.

What gear do I need to bring?

Trekking boots, warm clothes, rain gear, backpack, water bottles, and headlamp. We provide a detailed packing list after booking.

Can I rent equipment from you?

Yes, we provide tents, sleeping bags, jackets, and trekking poles on rent. Advance booking required with additional charges.

What type of food is provided on the trek?

Complete vegetarian meals including breakfast, lunch, snacks and dinner. All meals are freshly prepared by experienced cooks with quality ingredients.

What if I get sick during the trek?

Our trained guides carry first aid kits and can handle basic medical issues. Serious cases get immediate evacuation to nearest medical facility.

What is your cancellation policy?

Cancellations 15+ days before trek get 90% refund. 7-14 days get 50% refund. Less than 7 days get no refund. Weather cancellations get full refund.

Where do we stay during the trek?

High-quality camping tents (shared basis), local guesthouses, or homestays depending on the specific trek route and itinerary.

What is the group size?

Minimum 6 people, maximum 15 people per group to ensure safety and personalized attention from our guides.

Is transportation to base camp included?

Yes, pickup and drop from nearest major town or railway station is included. Exact pickup points shared after booking confirmation.
